# Care and Repair

Gear lasts longer when it's cared for. These are the basics.

## Waterproof shells (Summit Shell Jacket, Torrent Rain Pants)

Wash every 10–15 wears with a technical detergent (Nikwax Tech Wash or similar). Do not use regular laundry detergent — surfactants in standard detergent degrade the DWR (durable water repellent) finish. Tumble dry low to reactivate the DWR. Reapply a spray-on DWR once or twice a season if water stops beading on the face fabric.

## Down insulation (Ember Down Hoodie 800FP, Cascade 20°F Sleeping Bag)

Wash sparingly — once or twice a year at most. Use a down-specific wash (Nikwax Down Wash or Grangers), a front-loading machine, and the gentle cycle. Dry on low with three clean tennis balls to break up clumps. Expect a 2–3 hour dry cycle; pull early and only when fully dry inside.

## Merino wool (Glacier Merino Base Layer)

Hand wash or use the wool cycle with wool-safe detergent. Lay flat to dry. Do not tumble dry — heat shrinks merino permanently.

## Tents (Cirrus Ultralight Tent 2P, Basecamp Dome Tent 4P)

Pitch at home after trips to dry before storage. Store loosely stuffed, not rolled tight. Spot-clean with a soft sponge and water only — detergents degrade the silicone and polyurethane coatings.

## Cast iron (Campfire Cast Iron Skillet 10in)

Never use soap. Rinse with hot water, scrub with a stiff brush or chainmail scrubber, dry immediately on a burner, and wipe a thin coat of neutral oil onto the cooking surface.
